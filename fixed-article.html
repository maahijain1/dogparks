Service Overview & Process

Modern kennel screening isn't a single test but a comprehensive process designed to evaluate multiple aspects of canine behavior. The goal is to create safe, compatible playgroups while identifying potential aggression triggers before problems occur.

What's Involved in the Screening

Professional screening typically includes:

  • Pre-screening questionnaire about the dog's history and behavior
  • One-on-one evaluation with a trained behavior specialist
  • Controlled introductions to neutral dogs
  • Observation in small playgroups
  • Resource guarding assessment
  • Handling sensitivity evaluation

Screening Process & Methodology

The design and implementation of screening protocols vary significantly between facilities, but the most effective ones share common elements focused on safety and accurate assessment.

Evaluation Environment

Top-tier kennels create specialized evaluation areas that minimize stress while allowing for controlled observations. These spaces typically feature:

  • Neutral territory to prevent territorial behavior
  • Multiple entry/exit points to manage introductions
  • Visual barriers that can be adjusted as needed
  • Minimal distractions during initial assessment
  • Safety equipment readily available

Staff Training and Expertise

The quality of screening depends heavily on staff expertise. In 2025, leading facilities require:

  • Certification in canine behavior assessment
  • Minimum 40 hours of hands-on training
  • Regular continuing education on dog body language
  • CPR and first aid certification for dogs

Pros and Cons

What We Loved

  • Significantly enhanced safety: Proper screening reduces incidents by over 80%
  • Better matched playgroups: Dogs with similar play styles enjoy their time more
  • Educational value: Owners learn about their dog's behavior and needs
  • Early problem detection: Potential issues are identified before they escalate
  • Reduced stress: Appropriately placed dogs show lower stress indicators

Areas for Improvement

  • Time investment: Comprehensive screening can take several hours
  • Cost: Advanced screening may increase service prices
  • Availability: Not all facilities offer thorough assessments
  • False positives: Occasionally, shy dogs may be misclassified as incompatible
  • Staff training requirements: Effective screening requires significant staff education

Purchase Recommendations

Best For:

  • Dog owners prioritizing safety above all else
  • Dogs with previous negative group experiences
  • Puppies needing proper socialization foundations
  • Dogs with specific play style preferences
  • Owners who want to understand their dog's behavior better

Skip If:

  • You need immediate daycare with no waiting period
  • Your budget doesn't allow for premium services
  • Your dog has documented severe aggression issues (seek specialized behaviorist instead)
  • You're only looking for occasional, unsupervised play

Alternatives to Consider:

  • Basic screening facilities: Lower cost but reduced safety assurance
  • Private play sessions: One-on-one or very small group interactions
  • Structured training classes: Supervised socialization with professional guidance
  • In-home pet sitters: Avoids group settings entirely
