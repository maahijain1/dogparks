Service Overview & Process

Modern kennel screening isn't a single test but a comprehensive process designed to evaluate multiple aspects of canine behavior. The goal is to create safe, compatible playgroups while identifying potential aggression triggers before problems occur.

What's Involved in the Screening

Professional screening typically includes:

  • Pre-screening questionnaire about the dog's history and behavior
  • One-on-one evaluation with a trained behavior specialist
  • Controlled introductions to neutral dogs
  • Observation in small playgroups
  • Resource guarding assessment
  • Handling sensitivity evaluation

Screening Process & Methodology

The design and implementation of screening protocols vary significantly between facilities, but the most effective ones share common elements focused on safety and accurate assessment.

Evaluation Environment

Top-tier kennels create specialized evaluation areas that minimize stress while allowing for controlled observations. These spaces typically feature:

  • Neutral territory to prevent territorial behavior
  • Multiple entry/exit points to manage introductions
  • Visual barriers that can be adjusted as needed
  • Minimal distractions during initial assessment
  • Safety equipment readily available
